Buy & Sell Safely

CarGone NZ connects you directly with other Kiwis. A few simple precautions keep every deal smooth, fair and safe for both sides.

💰 For Buyers

  • ✓Inspect before you pay. See the vehicle in person, or arrange an independent inspection, before handing over any money.
  • ✓Verify the vehicle. Check the rego, WOF and ownership on the NZTA website, and confirm the plate and VIN match the car.
  • ✓Meet in a safe, public place. Daytime, with someone you trust, is best — especially for cash deals.
  • ✓Never pay a deposit to “hold” a car. Be wary of anyone who refuses to meet or pushes for upfront payment.

🚗 For Sellers

  • ✓Confirm payment first. Count cash, or wait until a transfer actually lands in your account, before releasing the vehicle.
  • ✓Notify the change of ownership. Tell NZTA you’ve sold or disposed of the vehicle so you’re no longer liable for it.
  • ✓Remove your belongings & data. Clear the glovebox and boot, and unpair your phone from the car’s system.
  • ✓Keep a record. Note the buyer’s name and the date, and keep a copy of your listing for your records.
⚠️CarGone NZ is a marketplace, not a party to your sale. We don’t take payment or handle vehicles on your behalf, so always deal directly, trust your instincts, and walk away from anything that feels off. More in our FAQ.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it free to list my car?+
Yes — listing a vehicle on CarGone NZ is completely free, whether you want to sell it, part it out, or get rid of it as scrap. There are no fees to post a listing.
Do you take cars that don’t run, or have no WOF or rego?+
Absolutely. Damaged, non-running, written-off and deregistered vehicles are all welcome. List it as “Get rid of it (free/scrap)” or “Sell the parts” and you’ll still connect with people who want it.
How does removal work?+
Once you and a buyer agree, removal is arranged at a time that suits you — often same day. Many scrap and parts buyers will collect the vehicle directly from your driveway, garage or the roadside, anywhere in New Zealand.
When and how do I get paid?+
Payment is arranged directly between you and the buyer, and for scrap and cash sales is usually paid in person when the vehicle is collected. Always confirm the money has cleared before releasing the car.
What areas do you cover?+
CarGone NZ is nationwide. You’ll find buyers and sellers across Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch, Hamilton, Tauranga, Dunedin, Palmerston North and everywhere in between.
Do I need to tell NZTA when I sell or scrap my car?+
Yes. To make sure you’re no longer responsible for the vehicle, notify NZTA that you’ve sold or disposed of it (a change of ownership, or a notice of disposal). You can do this online on the NZTA website.
Is my contact information safe?+
Enquiries reach you through the form on this site, and we only share what’s needed to connect a genuine buyer with a seller. See our Privacy Policy for the full detail on what we collect and why.
Can I sell individual parts instead of the whole car?+
Yes. Choose “Sell the parts” when you list, then add each part with its own price. It’s a great way to recover real value from an end-of-life vehicle.
